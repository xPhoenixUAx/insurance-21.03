Uninsured Motorist Coverage

Uninsured motorist coverage is often compared by drivers who want added protection if another driver has no insurance, and in some cases if the other driver’s coverage is not enough to pay for the loss.

State rules can differOften reviewed with liability limitsCan include UM and UIM options

Comparison focus

Users often compare state availability, bodily injury limits, and whether underinsured motorist coverage is offered alongside it.

This is one of the more state-sensitive coverage types, so quote context matters.

What it covers

Uninsured motorist comparisons are often driven by exposure to uninsured or underinsured drivers.

Depending on the state and provider, uninsured motorist protection may involve bodily injury coverage, and sometimes property damage. Some users also compare underinsured motorist coverage when they want protection against low-limit policies held by other drivers.

Because the rules vary across states, drivers often need to review local availability and policy structure before comparing quotes effectively.

What drivers usually review

  • State-specific UM and UIM rules
  • Available bodily injury limits
  • Whether property damage options are offered
  • How the added protection changes total quote cost

Protection overlap

How users compare

Uninsured motorist comparisons usually happen in a state-by-state context.

Users often want to know whether this coverage is required, optional, or paired with underinsured motorist coverage in their market. That makes provider comparison especially useful, because availability and structure may not be consistent from one quote path to another.

Rather than making assumptions, many drivers compare quote options with the exact state context and limit preferences they care about.
