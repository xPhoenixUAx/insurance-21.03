Uninsured Motorist Insurance

Uninsured motorist coverage is often compared by drivers who want added protection when another driver has no insurance or not enough coverage to pay for losses.

What this coverage can involve

Depending on the state and provider, uninsured or underinsured motorist coverage may apply to bodily injury costs and, in some markets, property damage claims.

Factors drivers often weigh

State requirements, local uninsured driver rates, existing health coverage, and whether the policy offers underinsured motorist options all shape comparison choices.

How comparison usually happens

Drivers commonly compare state-specific availability, coverage limits, and how adding this protection changes total quote costs across providers.

Important to compare

  • State rules for UM/UIM coverage
  • Available bodily injury and property damage limits
  • How added protection affects total premium
