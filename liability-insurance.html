Liability Insurance

Liability insurance is one of the most common starting points when comparing auto insurance because many states require minimum limits before a vehicle can be legally driven.

What this coverage is often meant to address

Liability insurance generally focuses on third-party costs after an at-fault accident, including bodily injury and property damage up to policy limits.

Factors drivers often compare

Users commonly look at state minimums, preferred liability limits, household asset protection goals, and how provider pricing changes at different limit levels.

How comparison usually works

Drivers often request quotes from several providers, compare legal minimum options against higher-limit alternatives, and review how those choices fit their budget.

Good to review

  • Your state's minimum liability requirements
  • Preferred bodily injury and property damage limits
  • Whether a bundled policy changes quote options
