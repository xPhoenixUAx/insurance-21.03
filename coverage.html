Coverage overview

Review Auto Insurance Coverage Options Before You Compare

Use this guide to understand how common coverage types work, what drivers often compare, and which pages to visit before requesting quotes.

Built for comparison, not direct policy sales

DriveQuote Compare helps users explore coverage options and connect with licensed insurance companies. The platform does not underwrite policies or make carrier recommendations.

What to compare

  • Required coverages in your state
  • Vehicle value and repair considerations
  • Deductible preferences
  • Driver history and household needs

Liability Insurance

Usually forms the legal baseline for driving in many states and may help cover third-party bodily injury or property damage claims.

Explore liability insurance

Full Coverage

A comparison term often used for policies that combine liability coverage with physical damage protections like collision and comprehensive.

Explore full coverage

Collision Insurance

Often reviewed by drivers with newer vehicles, loans, or leases who want to compare repair cost protection after collisions.

Explore collision insurance

Comprehensive Insurance

Can be relevant when comparing protection for theft, weather events, animal strikes, falling objects, and other non-collision losses.

Explore comprehensive insurance

Uninsured Motorist

Comparison needs may vary by state because uninsured and underinsured motorist rules are not the same everywhere.

Explore uninsured motorist insurance

SR-22 Insurance Filings

For drivers who need proof of financial responsibility, the comparison process often centers on filing availability and state requirements.

Explore SR-22 insurance
